Glossary of Terms
Essential definitions for understanding B2B pharmaceutical e-commerce

Core Business Terms

Provider

A medical practice, clinic, or healthcare facility that purchases peptides from Viora Biotech. Must have an active NPI number to qualify. Think of them as your B2B customers.

Sales Rep (Rep)

A sales representative who brings in providers and earns commission on their purchases. Each provider is "attributed" to a rep, meaning that rep gets credit (and commission) for all the provider's orders.

Super Admin

The highest level of access in the system. Can create providers, assign reps, manage products, process orders, and view all reports. This is typically the business owner or operations manager.

Rep Attribution

The automatic linking of a sales rep to a provider and all their orders. This ensures reps get credit and commission for the customers they brought in. It's like a permanent "tag" that says "this customer belongs to Rep A."

B2B (Business-to-Business)

A business model where you sell to other businesses, not individual consumers. In this case, Viora Biotech sells to medical clinics, not to individual patients.

Medical & Compliance Terms

NPI (National Provider Identifier)

A unique 10-digit ID number for all healthcare providers in the United States. It's like a social security number for doctors and clinics. We require this to verify that customers are legitimate medical practices, not random individuals.

Peptide

Short chains of amino acids (building blocks of proteins) used in medical treatments. Examples include peptides for weight loss, muscle growth, anti-aging, etc. These are pharmaceutical-grade products.

COA (Certificate of Analysis)

An official document from a lab that confirms the purity, potency, and safety of a product. Think of it as a "quality report card" for each peptide. Medical providers need this to verify they're buying legitimate, safe products.

Third-Party Testing Data

Lab results from an independent testing facility (not the manufacturer) that verify product quality. This adds extra credibility and trust.

Vial

A small glass container that holds liquid medication (in this case, peptides). Vial size is measured in milliliters (ml), like "10ml vial."

Dosage

The amount of active ingredient in a product, measured in milligrams (mg). For example, "5mg peptide" means there are 5 milligrams of the peptide per dose.

E-commerce & Ordering Terms

Tiered Pricing

Volume discounts where the price per unit decreases as you buy more. Example: 1-24 units cost $150 each, but 25-99 units cost $125 each. The more you buy, the cheaper each unit becomes.

SKU (Stock Keeping Unit)

A unique code for each product variant. Think of it as a product's "ID number." For example, "Peptide X 5mg 10ml vial" would have a different SKU than "Peptide X 10mg 10ml vial."

Minimum Order Quantity (MOQ)

The smallest amount of a product you can order. For example, if the MOQ is 10 vials, you must buy at least 10 vials; you can't buy just 5.

Discount Code

A promotional code (like "WELCOME10") that providers can enter at checkout to get a discount. Can be percentage-based (10% off) or fixed amount ($50 off).

Cart

The virtual shopping basket where providers add products before purchasing. The cart automatically calculates totals, applies tiered pricing, and shows discounts.

Checkout

The final step where providers enter shipping info, select payment method, and complete their purchase. Like the "cashier" in a physical store.

Payment & Financial Terms

Tycoon Payments

The payment processing platform that handles all transactions for Viora Biotech. Tycoon manages the entire payment integration including credit cards, debit cards, and ACH bank transfers. All payment processing, security, and compliance is handled by Tycoon on their end.

ACH (Automated Clearing House)

A bank-to-bank payment method (like electronic checks). It's cheaper than credit cards but takes 3-5 business days to process. Good for large B2B purchases.

Commission

The percentage of sales that a rep earns as payment. For example, if a rep has a 15% commission rate and their provider spends $1,000, the rep earns $150.

Commission Rate

The percentage used to calculate commission. Can vary per rep (e.g., Rep A gets 15%, Rep B gets 20%). Set by the admin.

Payout

The actual payment given to a rep for their earned commissions. In V1, this is done manually (admin generates report, processes payment via Gusto, marks as "Paid").

Gusto

A payroll and HR software used to pay employees and contractors. In this case, it's used to process commission payouts to sales reps.

System & Technical Terms

Invite-Only System

A registration model where new users can't sign up on their own. They must receive an email invitation from an admin. This ensures only verified medical practices get access.

Activation Link

A unique URL sent in the invite email that allows a provider to set their password and activate their account. Usually expires after 7 days for security.

Toast Notification

A small pop-up message that appears briefly on screen to confirm an action (like "Provider created successfully"). It "pops up" like toast from a toaster, then disappears.

Modal

A pop-up window that appears over the main screen, requiring the user to interact with it before continuing. Used for detailed views (like order details) or confirmations (like "Are you sure you want to delete?").

CSV Export

Downloading data as a spreadsheet file (Comma-Separated Values) that can be opened in Excel or Google Sheets. Used for commission reports, order lists, etc.

Badge

A small colored circle with a number that appears on navigation items to show pending items. For example, "Pending Applications (3)" where the "3" is in a red badge.

Bulk Actions

Performing the same action on multiple items at once. For example, selecting 5 applications and approving them all with one click, instead of approving each one individually.

Filter

A way to narrow down a list by specific criteria. For example, filtering orders to show only "Shipped" orders, or filtering providers to show only those assigned to "Rep A."

Dropdown

A menu that "drops down" when you click it, showing a list of options to choose from. Like selecting a rep from a list of all reps.

Order & Fulfillment Terms

Order Status

The current stage of an order in the fulfillment process. Typical stages: Pending (just placed) â†’ Processing (being prepared) â†’ Shipped (on the way) â†’ Delivered (received by customer).

Tracking Number

A unique code (like "1Z999AA10123456784") from the shipping carrier (FedEx, UPS, USPS) that lets customers track their package's location in real-time.

Fulfillment

The process of receiving an order, packing it, and shipping it to the customer. "Order fulfillment" means completing all these steps.

Invoice

A detailed bill showing what was ordered, how much it cost, and payment details. Sent to the provider after purchase. Used for accounting and tax purposes.

Refund

Returning money to a customer, typically due to a cancelled order, returned product, or billing error. Processed through the same payment method used for the original purchase.

Reorder

A quick way to purchase the same items from a previous order. The system automatically adds those items to the cart with current pricing (not the old prices).

Low Stock Alert

A notification that warns when a product's inventory is running low (e.g., below 20 units). Helps prevent stockouts (running out of product completely).

Report & Analytics Terms

Date Range

The time period you want to analyze. For example, "Nov 1-15, 2025" or "Last 30 days." Used in reports to see data for specific timeframes.

Dashboard

The main screen that shows an overview of key metrics and quick access to important features. Think of it as the "home page" for admins.

Sales Report

A document showing sales data, broken down by rep, provider, product, or time period. Used to analyze business performance and identify trends.

Top Performers

The reps, providers, or products with the highest sales in a given period. For example, "Top 10 providers by spend this month."

Average Order Value (AOV)

The average dollar amount spent per order. Calculated by dividing total revenue by number of orders. If you made $10,000 from 20 orders, AOV is $500.

YTD (Year-to-Date)

Data from the beginning of the current year until now. For example, "YTD sales" in November means sales from January 1 to November 30.

Business License & Legal Terms

Business License

An official government document that permits a business to operate legally. Required during provider application to verify the clinic is legitimate.

Reseller Certificate

A document that allows a business to buy products tax-free because they plan to resell them (not use them personally). Clinics that resell peptides to patients need this.

Application Status

The current stage of a provider's application: Pending (under review), Approved (accepted), Rejected (denied), or More Info Needed (requires additional documents).

Compliance

Following all laws and regulations related to pharmaceutical sales. Includes verifying NPIs, checking business licenses, maintaining COAs, etc.
